Samrat Prithviraj Chauhan

The Great King

(1177 - 1192)


Born and Early Life:

Prithviraj was born to the Chahamana king Someshvara and queen Karpuradevi (a Kalachuri princess).Both Prithviraj and his younger brother Hariraja were born in Gujarat, where their father Someshvara was brought up at the Chaulukya court by his maternal relatives.According to Prithviraja Vijaya, Prithviraj was born on the 12th day of the Jyeshtha month. The text does not mention the year of his birth, but provides some of the astrological planetary positions at the time of his birth, calling them auspicious. Based on these positions and assuming certain other planetary positions, Dasharatha Sharma calculated the year of Prithviraj's birth as 1166 CE. The medieval biographies of Prithviraj suggest that he was educated well. The Prithviraja Vijaya states that he mastered 6 languages; the Prithviraj Raso claims that he learned 14 languages, which appears to be an exaggeration. The Raso goes on to claim that he became well-versed in a number of subjects, including history, mathematics, medicine, military, painting, philosophy (mimamsa), and theology. Both the texts state that he was particularly proficient in archery.

He fought many battles during his reign, including the famous Battles of Tarain (1191 and 1192) against the Ghurids. He also fought against the Chandelas, Paramaras, and Tomars, among others. Prithviraj Chauhan's battles were known for their strategic planning and skilled use of cavalry, archery, and other weapons and so more..

The main battle of Samrat Prithviraj Chauhan was the Second Battle of Tarain, which took place in 1192 CE. In this battle, Prithviraj Chauhan faced off against the forces of Muhammad of Ghor, who had invaded India from present-day Afghanistan. Prithviraj's army was outnumbered, but he managed to defeat the enemy through clever tactics, including a feigned retreat that drew Muhammad's forces into a trap. However, Prithviraj was later captured and executed by Muhammad, marking the end of his reign..

Prithviraj Chauhan is known for his military achievements and his valor in battles. He fought several battles against his rival Rajput clans and also against the Muslim invaders who were attempting to conquer India. The history of Samrat Prithviraj Chauhan is also intertwined with the larger history of medieval India. His reign saw both conflict and cultural growth, and his legacy as a brave and skilled warrior king endures to this day.

Death Of Samrat Prithviraj Chauhan:

death of samrat prithviraj chauhan Samrat Prithviraj Chauhan, the legendary Rajput king, died in 1192 CE. He was captured by his arch-rival, Muhammad of Ghor, after the Second Battle of Tarain, which took place near present-day Haryana in northern India. Muhammad had invaded India with the aim of conquering it and had already defeated Prithviraj once in the First Battle of Tarain in 1191 CE. After his defeat in the Second Battle of Tarain, Prithviraj was taken prisoner by Muhammad of Ghor, who blinded him and took him to his capital in Ghazni, present-day Afghanistan. Legend has it that Prithviraj and his loyal friend and companion, Chand Bardai, later avenged his capture by using his skills as an archer to defeat Muhammad in a game of archery and kill him. However, the actual circumstances of Prithviraj's death are uncertain and have been the subject of much debate among historians and scholars. Nevertheless, his life and legacy as a brave and skilled warrior king continue to inspire and captivate people to this day.
